About Caesar Cipher
Caesar cipher shifts each letter in the alphabet by a fixed number of positions. With a shift of 3: A becomes D, B becomes E, Z wraps around to C. Numbers and special characters stay unchanged. There are only 25 possible shifts -- try each one until the plaintext makes sense.
Decode online with CyberChef at gchq.github.io/CyberChef using the "ROT13" or "Caesar Cipher Decode" recipe, or use any Caesar solver. On Linux: echo "CIPHERTEXT" | tr 'A-Za-z' 'N-ZA-Mn-za-m' for ROT13, or write a one-liner with a custom shift.
Hints
Look at the challenge title. The cipher is named after Julius Caesar. He used it to protect military communications. The shift value he personally favoured is historically documented and is a single digit.
The shift is 3. Julius Caesar used ROT-3 in his own correspondence. Decode the ciphertext with shift 3 (subtract 3 from each letter) or use CyberChef with the Caesar Cipher Decode recipe and shift set to 3.
