What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences and keep you logged in between sessions. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide a better user experience.

How AlexCyberX Uses Cookies

AlexCyberX uses only strictly necessary cookies. We do not use advertising cookies, tracking cookies, or third-party analytics cookies. Our cookies are used solely for the following purposes:

  • Session management — to keep you logged in while you browse the platform
  • Authentication — to verify your identity securely via Supabase
  • Language preference — to remember your selected display language
  • Cookie consent — to remember that you have acknowledged this notice

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies — These are essential for the platform to function. They cannot be disabled without affecting core features such as login and session continuity.
  • Functional Cookies — These remember your preferences such as language selection to improve your experience.

We do not use performance, analytics, marketing, or third-party cookies of any kind.

Third-Party Cookies

Our platform uses Supabase for authentication and Netlify for hosting. These services may set their own strictly necessary cookies as part of their operation. We do not control these cookies directly, but they are limited to functional use only. Please refer to their respective privacy policies for more details.

How to Manage Cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View cookies currently stored on your device
  • Delete existing cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all third-party cookies

Please note that disabling cookies may prevent you from logging in or using certain features of the platform. Deleting session cookies will log you out.

Cookie Retention

Session cookies are deleted automatically when you close your browser. Persistent cookies such as language preference are stored for up to 30 days and are then automatically removed.
